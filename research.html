Research Publications

Papers in cryptography, lightweight block ciphers, and secure edge computing

NES: A Novel Lightweight Block Cipher for Resource-Constrained Environments

Nilotpal Guha, Hiranmoy Roy, Debotosh Bhattacharjee

IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security (IEEE TIFS)

Submitted — 2026

This paper presents NES (Novel Encryption Standard), a lightweight block cipher designed for IoT and edge computing environments. NES achieves a favorable security-to-cost ratio with a 64-bit block size and 128-bit key, demonstrating resistance against differential and linear cryptanalysis while maintaining a low gate equivalent count suitable for hardware-constrained devices.

SESH OS: A Secure Edge Computing Operating System for IoT Networks

Nilotpal Guha, et al.

Under Review

Under Review — June 2026

This paper introduces SESH OS, a secure operating system designed for edge computing nodes in IoT networks. SESH OS implements hardware-backed secure boot, encrypted filesystem, and lightweight containerization to provide a hardened execution environment for edge workloads while maintaining minimal resource overhead.
